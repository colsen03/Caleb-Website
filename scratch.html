Marathon Running

Few things reveal character like mile 20 of a marathon. I have completed two full marathons — the Ogden Marathon (3:56:50) and the Big Cottonwood Heights Marathon (3:54:29) — and the sport has shaped how I approach challenges in finance and life: disciplined preparation, pacing strategy, and the mental fortitude to push through discomfort.

Training for a marathon mirrors building a financial model — both require a plan, iteration, and the ability to course-correct when projections don't match reality. I currently train 5 days a week and am targeting a sub-3:45 finish in my next race.

Why I Love Finance

Finance isn't just my major — it's how I think. Here are the key areas I'm most passionate about, and what draws me to each:

  1. Mergers & Acquisitions
    • The thrill of identifying undervalued targets
    • Complex deal structuring and negotiation dynamics
    • Synthesizing diligence into a single investment decision
  2. Leveraged Buyouts (LBOs)
    • Building detailed financial models from scratch (10-K, projections, debt schedules)
    • Understanding how leverage amplifies both risk and return
    • Competed in two LBO case competitions at BYU in 2026
  3. Private Equity
    • Long-term value creation beyond a single quarter
    • Operational improvement alongside financial engineering
    • Active member of BYU's PE/VC Association
  4. Entrepreneurship Through Acquisition (ETA)
    • Currently supporting ETA clients at Waystation Advisors
    • Identifying small-business acquisition targets in the PT/Med Spa vertical

Leadership & Service

Leadership is something I've taken seriously since earning my Eagle Scout — a project that remediated the Fremont, California Adult School Campus. Later, I led a team of 31 volunteer representatives for two years, an experience that sharpened my ability to inspire, delegate, and navigate diverse personalities.

Today I serve as Program Director at Y-Serve (BYU's student volunteer organization), leading recruitment for the Cougar Coaches sector. Whether leading a team of 4 interns at Global Ag or managing outreach campaigns, I believe great leadership is quiet, consistent, and focused on others' growth.
